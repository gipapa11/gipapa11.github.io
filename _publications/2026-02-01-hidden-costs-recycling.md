Exploiting the 2007 opening of 26 used-lead-acid-battery recycling plants in Kenya as a natural experiment, I estimate the causal effect of recycling-driven lead exposure on children's learning using individual national-exam records. Exposure lowers test scores by about 0.18 standard deviations, rivaling the gains from leading school-based interventions, with heterogeneity by gender and school type.
