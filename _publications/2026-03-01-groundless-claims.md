The property rights literature assumes that land continues to exist; climate change increasingly violates this assumption. This paper extends the Besley (1995) framework to settings where environmental destruction permanently eliminates the asset, replacing the standard investment and credit channels with three displacement-specific mechanisms: compensation receipt, adaptation investment, and post-displacement reallocation. Calibrated to Kenya's Rift Valley, where rising lakes have submerged over 100 km&sup2; since 2005.
